Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of Embodied AI, Vision-Language Models (VLMs), and Trustworthy Machine Learning. I am especially interested in building intelligent systems that can reason reliably and interact safely in complex physical environments.

  • Uncertainty Quantification & Safe AI

    Leveraging Conformal Prediction (CP) and uncertainty estimates to build trustworthy autonomous agents that know when to act and when to ask for human assistance.

  • Agent Infrastructure & Systems

    Building robust agent orchestration, tool-use evaluation pipelines, retrieval-augmented generation systems, and real-time execution architectures.

About Me

I am a Master's student in Artificial Intelligence Engineering (AIE) at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), graduating in December 2027. Prior to CMU, I received my B.E. in Robotics Engineering from Chongqing University (CQU) in 2026.

My research goal is to build reliable, uncertainty-aware, and interactive embodied AI systems that can seamlessly navigate and reason in complex, dynamic physical environments.
